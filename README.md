# Churn + LTV + ROI Decision System (Multi-Scenario Robust Marketing Simulation)

A production-style marketing decision simulation system that integrates:

- Churn prediction
- Two-stage LTV modeling
- Joint risk–value scoring
- Multi-level coupon policy optimization
- Multi-scenario uplift simulation
- Robust ROI-based budget selection

The system evaluates marketing strategies under structural uncertainty
(saturation, misalignment, noise, harm/cannibalization) and outputs
robust budget recommendations.

---

## 1️⃣ Project Objective

Instead of only predicting churn or LTV separately, this system:

1. Predicts user churn probability (`p_churn`)
2. Predicts future margin (`expected_margin`)
3. Constructs a joint risk–value score
4. Simulates realistic uplift mechanisms
5. Backtests multi-level coupon strategies
6. Selects optimal and robust budget allocation

Goal:

> Maximize net incremental profit under budget constraints,  
> while remaining robust across multiple real-world stress scenarios.

---

## 2️⃣ Pipeline Overview

### Step 1 – Event Construction
`make_events_online_retail.py`

Convert raw transaction data into user-week event format.

Outputs:
- revenue
- margin
- activity flags
- refund signals

---

### Step 2 – Feature Engineering
`build_features.py`

Construct lookback window features:

- RFM metrics
- Recency
- Activity trends
- Burst indicators
- Volatility measures

Generate multiple cutoff training samples.

---

### Step 3 – Churn Modeling
`train_churn.py`

Predict:
- Probability of churn in next 4 weeks

Calibration:
- `calibrate.py` (sigmoid / isotonic)

Output:
- `p_churn_cal`

---

### Step 4 – Two-Stage LTV Modeling
`train_ltv.py`

Stage 1:
- P(margin > 0)

Stage 2:
- E(margin | margin > 0)

Final:

E(margin) = P × E|pos



Output:
- `expected_margin_4w`

---

### Step 5 – Multi-Scenario Uplift Simulation
`simulate_uplift.py`

Construct a controllable “real world” uplift environment.

Scenarios include:

- `base` (normal world)
- `misalign` (uplift mismatched with score structure)
- `saturation` (diminishing marginal returns)
- `high_noise` (unstable environment)
- `harm` (cannibalization / adverse effect)

Outputs:
- True uplift
- True incremental gain for small / medium / large coupons
- Oracle best_coupon (upper bound benchmark)

---

### Step 6 – ROI Backtesting
`roi_backtest_sim.py`

Apply different strategies:

- score_churn
- score_value
- score_joint
- quantile_coupon
- best_coupon_by_prediction

For each:
- Select target fraction
- Assign coupon level
- Compute net gain
- Generate ROI curve

---

### Step 7 – Budget Optimization
`find_optimal_budget.py`

Select:

- Best strategy
- Best budget coverage (target_frac)

Supports:
- Single scenario
- Multi-scenario optimization
- Strategy filtering
- Budget constraints

---

### Step 8 – Robust Policy Recommendation
`summarize_robust_policy.py`

Across all scenarios:

- Compute mean performance
- Compute worst-case performance
- Select robust strategy
- Recommend robust budget (median optimal fraction)

Output:

robust_policy_summary.csv


---

## 3️⃣ Decision Architecture

The system separates:

### 1. Real World (Ground Truth)
Generated by simulation:
- true uplift
- true incremental gain

### 2. Decision World (Predictive View)
Available at decision time:
- p_churn_cal
- expected_margin

### 3. Evaluation Layer
- Backtest strategies
- Compute ROI
- Compare across stress scenarios

This prevents information leakage and enables structural robustness testing.

## 4️⃣ Key Insights

- Joint risk–value scoring outperforms pure churn baseline
- Value-driven strategy may dominate in value-skewed worlds
- Robust strategy should maximize worst-case net gain
- Budget optimality depends on structural assumptions
- Stress testing reveals sensitivity to misalignment and harm
